The Sport of Archery

Archery began as a tool for survival then warfare and sport combined, but this changed as firearms became the main ranged weapon used in war, causing archery to become fully an activity only used for fun. This shift finished many centuries ago in the year 1583, where in Finsbury, England, many believed the first large scale archery competition, for fun, happened.

True Sport Beginnings (16th-19th Century)

With gunpowder based weapons, the bow and arrow became obsolete as a primary weapon of war and this led to a big change. Archery changed from a military necessity to a fun activity, with Archery clubs and societies beginning to form in England, which held annual tournaments. The first large scale competition being in 1583 in Finsbury, England. In the 19th century it changed again to become a more formalized sport and the first Grand National Archery Society meeting was held in York in 1844, and the "York Round" (a series of shoots at different distances) became a standardized format.
